Our Story

It began quietly - the way most meaningful things do.

Three years ago, somewhere between the restless energy of Bangalore and the sleepless nights of New York, two lives - Alizeh Shireen and Raj Sanghvi - brushed against each other online. No expectations. No certainty. Just a conversation that refused to end.

They lived in different worlds - different time zones, different routines, different realities. And yet, in the quiet hours of the night, they found something familiar in each other.

There were days when the distance felt too heavy. Calls dropped. Messages delayed. Plans uncertain. Life, as it always does, tried to pull them in different directions.

And sometimes, nothing was said at all.

But somehow, they always found their way back.

Back to conversations that stretched for hours. Back to laughter that made the distance disappear. Back to a quiet understanding - the kind you don’t question, only feel.

What they built wasn’t loud or hurried. It was slow. Intentional. Patient. A kind of love that doesn’t ask to be seen - but quietly becomes the most important part of your day.

Hezila Café was born from that feeling. A space imagined in between conversations. In shared silence. In the in-between moments no one talks about, but everyone remembers.

It is not just a café. It is a reflection of finding peace in another person - even when the world feels uncertain. A place where time slows down. Where moments are meant to be felt, not rushed. Where every cup carries warmth - the kind that stays long after you leave.

Because at the end of it all, no matter how far life took them, they always found the same thing waiting -

Each other.
